    <h2>What is Dhurma City?</h2>
    <h3>Dhurma is a historic town in Saudi Arabia’s Riyadh Province, located about 60–73 km west of Riyadh. It is known for its deep Najdi heritage, desert valleys, and continuous urban growth.</h3>

<h3>Landmarks of Dhurma</h3>
<ul>
<li>Al-Fargh Palace – A historic palace that reflects traditional Najdi architecture and the legacy of local rulers.
</li>

<li>Bu Huwamiyah Palace – Another heritage site showcasing the cultural richness of the region.
</li>

<li>Qarqara Village – An ancient settlement that preserves the memory of Dhurma’s early inhabitants.
</li>

<li>Al-Aziz Wells – Historic water sources that sustained life in the desert and symbolize the town’s agricultural past.
</li>

</ul>
<h3>The future of Dhurma</h3>
<h4>Dhurma, once known mainly for its historic palaces and valleys, is now positioned to become a strategic extension of Riyadh’s growth. The Dharma Project West Riyadh, launched by Ajlan Global, covers 19.6 million square meters and is designed as a modern urban model that blends tradition with innovation.
</h4>
